Hilton Head Island Motoring Festival & Concours d’Elegance names 2014 Pinnacle and Honored Collectors

Media Contact:
Lindsey Harrell
(843) 785-7469
lharrell@hhiconcours.com

Joseph and Margie Cassini featured to include the 2013 Pebble Beach Concours Best of Show; William and Christine Snyder recognized as key contributors to Hilton Head Island’s success

HILTON HEAD ISLAND, S.C. – The 2014 Hilton Head Island Motoring Festival & Concours d’Elegance will include major international names in automotive collecting and racing when the event returns for its 13th annual celebration Oct. 24 – Nov. 2. Among those names are esteemed collectors Joseph and Margie Cassini and William and Christine Snyder, selected respectively as this year’s Pinnacle and Honored Collectors. The Cassinis and Snyders, along with their award-winning collections, will be featured in the Motoring Midway, Nov. 1 and 2, 2014 at the Port Royal Golf Club on Hilton Head Island.
“We wanted to recognize the Cassinis as this year’s standout collectors for their contributions to the collector hobby and the Snyders for their personal, ongoing support of the Hilton Head Island Concours d’Elegance. With invitations accepted, we couldn’t be more pleased with the two incredible collections we will be highlighting this year,” said Carolyn Vanagel, the Festival’s President. 

Joseph and Margie Cassini join fellow Pinnacle Award recipients, Sam & Emily Mann.  To celebrate their role at the 2014 Hilton Head Island Motoring Festival & Concours d’Elegance and their first trip to the event, the Cassinis of West Orange, N.J. have chosen to showcase three cars in the Pinnacle Collector exhibit, including the very car that was awarded the 2013 Pebble Beach Best of Show winner:

1934 Packard Convertible Victoria
Representing the epitome of craftsmanship, this Packard was a car that cost upwards of $7,000, a high price tag considering the weekly pay for the men who built it was a mere $25. The Packard Twelve was one of the fastest cars money could buy with a 445.5cid engine that produced 160hp and 322lb/ft of torque. The car, purchased by the Cassinis in 2010, underwent a complete restoration in 2012 and made its debut at the 2013 Pebble Beach Concours d’Elegance where it received Best in Class honors and the Gwenn Graham Most Elegant Convertible award. Even more impressively, the car took home the title of Best of Show, the first American car to do so since 2007 and only the fourth Packard ever to win.

1931 Duesenberg Model J Tourster
Attributed with the expression, “It’s a Duesy,” this car, one of only eight with the body designed by Gordon Buehrig, started at $8,500, by far the most expensive car available in America at that time. This car, one of only a few retaining the original body, wooden framing, chassis, engine and major components, has been owned by several noted collectors and marque enthusiasts including Dana Bullock, owner of Bullock’s department stores, Clifford Heinz, a member of the H.J. Heinz family and Andy Granatelli, noted racing-team owner and STP automotive entrepreneur. 

1927 Isotta Fraschini 8A S Roadster
The true thoroughbred of Italian motorcars in its day, the Isotta Fraschini Tip 8A series offered 115 horsepower, a redesigned frame and suspension and Isotta’s highly regarded three-speed transmission originally guaranteeing the car could do 90mph. The chassis of an 8A alone was priced at $9,750 while coach built models demanded upwards of $20,000. This car features the rare Fleetwood coachwork also seen in the Valentino car that made a splash at the New York Automobile Salon in Nov. 1926.

The 2014 Honored Collector, recognized for continued support of the Hilton Head Island event, will feature William and Christine Snyder of Hudson, OH. “The Snyders have been actively involved since the early years of the event. They have participated as both an exhibitor and in helping to source and secure notable cars for the event. We would not be where we are today without his support and that of only a handful of others like them who have been with us since the beginning,” said Vanagel. William and Christine Snyder, as part of this honor, will be showcasing five cars to include: 

1930 Stutz MB/SC
One of only 10 supercharged Stutz cars ever built, the car was first purchased by William Snyder on his 16th birthday on Feb. 3, 1947. Needing cash for his fledgling company, Snyder sold the car in 1964 and repurchased it in 2004. This car is one of only three known to survive.

1931 Packard 845
This car is one of a handful of the body style built by Rollston, designed by Rudy Creteur. Of the perhaps 20 Rollston bodies built in 1931, the convertible Victoria was the star, keeping the company in business as the Town Car market had dried up.

1954 Chevrolet Corvette
This is one of only 400 Blue Corvettes built in 1954 and is equipped with both soft and removable hard tops. It has been restored to exact original specs.

1963 Ford Shorty
This is one of ten pre-production chassis. The wheelbase was shortened by 16 inches. The 260 ci motor was enlarged to 302 ci. In 1964 it was shown as part of a Ford travelling show. This car will be shown as part of the Mustang 50th Anniversary celebration in the Concours d’Elegance on Sunday, November 2, 2014.

1966 Ford Shelby GT 350
The advertised time for 0-60 for this model was 7.3 seconds with a top speed of 124 mph.

ABOUT THE HILTON HEAD ISLAND MOTORING FESTIVAL & CONCOURS D’ELEGANCE:

The Hilton Head Island Motoring Festival & Concours d’Elegance will celebrate its thirteenth year fall of 2014. The Festival kicks off with the Savannah Speed Classic, October 24 – 26, on the Grand Prize of America road course situated on the property of The Westin Savannah Harbor Golf Resort & Spa. The festivities will continue on Hilton Head Island, SC, November 1 – 2, at Port Royal Golf Club where the Concours d’Elegance (November 2) will take center stage. For more information, visit www.HHIMotoringFestival.com online.
